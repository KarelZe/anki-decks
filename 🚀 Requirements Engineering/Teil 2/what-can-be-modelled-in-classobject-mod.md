### Front
What can be modelled in class/object models? What can't?

### Back
<b>Can</b>
<ul><li>Objects as classes or abstract objects</li><li>Local properties as attributes</li><li>Relationships / non-local properties as associations</li><li>Services offered by objects as operations on objects or classes (called features in UML)</li></ul><b>Can't</b>
<ul><li>Object behaviour must be modelled in separate state machines in UML</li><li>System-context interfaces and functionality from a user's perspective can't be modelled adequately</li></ul>
